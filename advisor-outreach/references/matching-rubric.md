# Matching Rubric

Rank advisors by research fit first. School ranking is a secondary tie-breaker.

## Tier Definitions

A tier:

- The advisor's recent work directly overlaps with the user's research experience or target direction.
- There is concrete evidence from recent papers, projects, or lab descriptions.
- The user has at least one strong experience that can be naturally connected to the advisor's work.
- Contact first and write the most customized email.

B tier:

- The advisor's broad field matches, but the exact methods, disease area, data type, or application differs.
- The user's experience can be framed as transferable.
- Contact after A tier or in parallel if school/discipline/platform is especially attractive.

C tier:

- The advisor is only partially related, or evidence is thin.
- The school/platform is attractive but direction fit is uncertain.
- Use as exploration or backup; email should be cautious and less specific.

## Suggested Scoring

Use this only as a guide, not as a rigid formula:

- Research topic fit: 0-40
- Method/technical fit: 0-20
- Advisor activity and recent output: 0-15
- User evidence strength for this advisor: 0-15
- School/discipline/platform fit: 0-10

Recommended tier:

- A: 80-100
- B: 60-79
- C: 40-59
- Below 40: usually do not contact unless the user has a strategic reason.

## School Ordering

Within the same tier, sort by:

1. Discipline strength in the target direction.
2. Advisor/lab activity and student training fit.
3. School platform: 985, strong 211, Double First Class specialty university, ordinary 211, other.
4. Geographic preference.
5. Feasibility based on user's background.

Do not present a fixed 985/211 ranking as timeless fact. If ranking matters, search current and field-specific sources.
