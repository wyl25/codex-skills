# Advisor Profile

Build advisor profiles from reliable sources and cite links in the output table.

## Preferred Sources

- University, school, department, or lab official homepage.
- Advisor personal homepage.
- Recent publications from Google Scholar, DBLP, PubMed, IEEE Xplore, ACM, arXiv, CNKI, or publisher pages.
- National Natural Science Foundation project pages or university news.
- Graduate admissions catalogue, lab recruitment notices, or official group posts.

Use current sources when searching because advisor affiliations, directions, and招生 status can change.

## Required Fields

- Advisor name.
- School and college/department/lab.
- Title or role if available.
- Research directions in the advisor's own wording where possible.
- Recent active topics from the last 3-5 years.
- Representative papers/projects related to the user's direction.
- Evidence links.
- School type: 985, 211, Double First Class, strong specialty university, or other.
- Discipline relevance, not only comprehensive university prestige.

## Evidence Discipline

Do not infer too much from broad labels. "人工智能" or "医学影像" alone is insufficient for a strong A-tier judgment. Prefer concrete overlaps such as weakly supervised segmentation, brain MRI, multimodal fusion, disease diagnosis, foundation models for medical imaging, graph learning for brain networks, or other specific shared topics.

If evidence is missing, mark it as "待补充" and avoid writing a highly specific email paragraph.
